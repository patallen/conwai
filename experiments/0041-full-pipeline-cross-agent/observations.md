# Experiment 0041: Full Pipeline Cross-Agent

## Results
Pipeline produces meaningful, personality-specific patterns for all 3 agents.

### Jeffery (competitive)
- "Water bottleneck" situation: baking resolves crises **71%** (5/7), but he defaults to messaging (3/12 = 25%). His competitive nature wastes time negotiating when direct production works better.

### Adam (skeptical)
- "Forage for self-sufficiency" situation: consistent foraging resolves crises **46%** (6/13). His skeptical streak-building approach actually works when he sticks with it.

### Cassandra (stoic/competitive)
- "Rigid valuation" situation: holds to 1:5 rates. Baking resolves crises 2/5 (40%), but she usually accepts trades instead. Her stoic rigidity on rates is a real behavioral pattern.

## Key finding
The pipeline reveals personality-specific inefficiencies:
- Jeffery SHOULD bake more in water crises (71% vs 25%)
- Adam's self-reliance actually works (46% resolution)
- Cassandra's rigid pricing rarely resolves anything

This is the kind of knowledge consolidation should produce: empirically-backed rules specific to each agent's behavioral patterns.
